I am a MPhil student at the University of Cambridge funded by a Churchill Scholarship. I work with Carlos Caldas, Oscar Rueda, and Florian Markowetz on the immunogenomics of breast cancer. I previously recieved B.S. degrees in Biological Sciences and Economics from the University of Maryland in 2023.

I have been fortunate to work with an amazing set of researchers in the past. I studied RNA frameshifting with Jonathan Dinman and novel combinatorial radiation therapies with Hem Shukla at the University of Maryland. As an Amgen Scholar at Duke University under Zachary Reitman, I studied telomerase regulation in glioblastoma and developed METIS, a metabolic engineering tool built off oncogenomic data. I also worked at the University of Iowa Department of Emergency Medicine with Nicholas Mohr and the Johns Hopkins University Alzheimer's Disease Research Center with Tong Li.

I am primarily interested in developing cost-effective precision medicine pipelines for cancer. I also have tangential interests in pedagogy and health equity research. Outside of research, I am the founder of a startup with the mission of decarbonizing science. For more details, check my CV or hit me up on my email.
