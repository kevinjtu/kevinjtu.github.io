I am a MPhil student at the University of Cambridge funded by a Churchill Scholarship. I work with Oscar Rueda and Carlos Caldas on the functional genomics of breast cancer. I previously recieved B.S. degrees in Biological Sciences and Economics from the University of Maryland in 2023.

I have been fortunate to work with an amazing set of researchers in the past. I studied RNA frameshifting with Jonathan Dinman and novel combinatorial radiation therapies with Hem Shukla at the University of Maryland. As an Amgen Scholar at Duke University under Zachary Reitman, I studied telomerase regulation in glioblastoma and developed METIS, a metaolic engineering tool built off oncogenomic data. I also interned at University of Iowa with Nicholas Mohr and Johns Hopkins University with Tong Li.

I am primarily interested in developing cost-effective precision medicine pipelines for cancer. I also have tangential interests in science education and health equity research. Outside of research, I am involved in sustainability initiatives to decarbonize science. For more details, check my CV or hit me up on my email.
